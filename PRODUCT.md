# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone who relies on external services and does not want to manually check status pages. Primary contexts: developers and engineers who want instant awareness of breakages while they work; IT and ops teams doing a quick glance across services; indie hackers and power users running their own stack. All share the same job: know the moment something goes down, without friction.

## Product Purpose

UpCheck is a lightweight cross-platform desktop app that monitors the real-time status of external services and alerts the user the moment something changes. It lives quietly in the system tray and gets out of the way. Success looks like: the user never has to open a browser to check a status page again.

## Brand Personality

Clean, modern, professional. Calm and trustworthy. The app earns attention by withholding it -- it is silent until it matters.

## Anti-references

- Generic dark dev tools (VS Code clones, terminal emulator aesthetics, nerd-font iconography)
- Busy SaaS dashboards with widget overload, chart grids, or metric cards for their own sake
- Enterprise monitoring tools (Datadog, Grafana) -- no complexity or information density as a signal of power

## Design Principles

1. **Silence is the feature.** The UI should feel ambient and calm at rest. Visual weight is earned by status change, not decoration.
2. **One glance is enough.** Every screen should communicate its key information without scanning or reading.
3. **Professional without being corporate.** Polished and trustworthy, not stiff or overdesigned.
4. **Restraint over richness.** When in doubt, subtract. Empty space is not a problem to fill.
5. **Dark done differently.** The dark theme should feel considered, not defaulted to -- avoid the generic dev-tool aesthetic by focusing on refined color, spacing, and type choices.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA baseline. Keyboard navigable controls. Sufficient contrast on all text. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations.
